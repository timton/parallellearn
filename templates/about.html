Welcome to Parallellearn

The idea of this website came to me while learning French. The Internet is wide and vast - you have all the resources in the world to learn a foreign language, even if you're doing it all by yourself - from textbooks, to applications like Rosetta Stone, to audio podcasts (ex. Pimsleur), to websites (Duolingo, Memrise) etc.

It was all rather straightforward, but personally, I hit a glass ceiling at a certain point. Without the immediate chance to move to an environment where people around you natively speak this newly-learnt language of yours (i.e. immersion), I found it pretty hard making that leap from an intermediate level to advanced, fluent French.

The reading and listening comprehensions were constantly improving, but my ability to express myself freely in French was stalling. Now, that's something normal when you're starting, but I couldn't find any way to perceptibly get better from this point of view. Listening to French songs, watching French movies, all fine and good, but I was willing to practice my translation skills, and I found a distinct lack of resources in this area - real-life conversations like, not the Duolingo-style ("The elephant has two apples") type of thing (don't get me wrong, Duolingo is awesome in its own right, but I found it lacking from this perspective). Specifically I was looking for books with parallel translations, transcript exercises, and, naturally, translation exercises. And I didn't find anything out there to satisfy my needs.

Enter this website. Ultimately, what I'm trying to achieve with it is:

  • having well-known artworks (movies, books, songs) in multiple languages and giving users the opportunity to exercise and improve their translation skills, especially when translating real-life dialogue, from everyday situations;
  • having the audio version of the very same art-works (movies with their original / dubbed soundtrack, audio books, songs) and giving users the opportunity to exercise their transcripting and/or translation skills;
  • giving users the possibility to download those artworks (especially books) in a parallel-translation version, so that if you want to read in a foreign language and you don't understand something, you get the context translation, not the rigid dictionary one;

So far I've only achieved the first of the three stated goals, but hopefully I will manage to get all three done, before laziness/lack of interes wins me over. Of course, this cannot be done without your help. By giving you the possibility to contribute, we can help each other out learn new languages / improve our foreign language skills.

Enjoy!
